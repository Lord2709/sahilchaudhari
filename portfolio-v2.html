About Me

Building robust systems from data to deployment

I am Sahil Chaudhari, a Master's student in Applied Machine Learning at the University of Maryland, College Park, with a strong interest in building reliable and scalable ML systems. My work focuses on taking models beyond experimentation, from data preprocessing and feature engineering to evaluation, optimization, and deployment.

I have worked on projects ranging from backtesting quantitative trading strategies to building a fully local Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system. During internships, I applied geospatial clustering, automated decision systems, and large language models to solve real operational and analytical problems.

I enjoy understanding how models behave under the hood, improving them iteratively, and building solutions that balance performance with practical constraints. Outside work, I play guitar and follow anime and manga.
